<h4>English translations</h4>
From the Pāli (Bodhi 1995)

<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha is visited by a non-Buddhist, who asserts that physical form (rupa) is the most supreme. The Buddha challenges this assertion by showing how light can outshine light, and then argues that attachment to the five senses leads to lust and desire. Vemasuku, initially angry and defiant, is eventually converted and gains insight into the Dharma. He then requests to become a monk and ultimately attains enlightenment.
