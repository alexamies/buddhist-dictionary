<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha, surrounded by a vast assembly of monks, bodhisattvas, and other beings, enters a profound meditative state (samadhi) that makes his physical form disappear. This event prompts questions from bodhisattvas like Maitreya, who seek to understand the nature of the Buddha's samadhi. The Buddha reveals that this samadhi is difficult to encounter and comprehend, but those who embrace it and the wisdom it represents will swiftly attain enlightenment and Buddhahood, while those who doubt it will suffer greatly. Embracing the teaching brings great blessings, leading to celestial and earthly rebirths, and ultimately Buddhahood.
