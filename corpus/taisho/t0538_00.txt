<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha is asked about the merits of a man named Kahuo Anahan, who is known for teaching and leading his disciples. Kahuo Anahan explains that he follows four principles: giving to others, speaking good words, caring for his fellow students, and sharing resources. The Buddha praises these principles and later adds eight more virtues Kahuo Anahan possesses, centered around humility and not seeking recognition for his good deeds.
