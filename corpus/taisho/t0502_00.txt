<h4>Summary</h4>
While residing in a monastery, the Buddha instructs younger monks with Dharma talks. Subsequently, senior monks take responsibility for guiding and teaching these younger and newly arrived monks. The Buddha praises the monks for their virtuous practice and explains that past and future Buddhas have similar monastic communities. He describes the various levels of spiritual attainment achieved by monks in the community, emphasizing the diverse practices leading to enlightenment.
