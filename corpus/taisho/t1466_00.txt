<h4>Summary</h4>
In this text, the wise Upali asks the Buddha about the qualifications of a monk who is worthy to be relied upon throughout their life. The Buddha explains that a monk who lacks knowledge of the monastic code, including the rules of confession, and is less than five years ordained is not reliable. Conversely, a monk who possesses this knowledge and is beyond five years ordained is worthy of reliance. The text then details several specific aspects of monastic discipline, highlighting the importance of understanding the rules regarding offenses and the proper procedures for following the monastic code.
