<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha, while at Vulture Peak Mountain, expounds on the qualities that lead a Bodhisattva to either lose or gain insight, forget their aspiration, or diminish or increase their virtuous actions. He explains how Bodhisattvas should cultivate certain virtues, avoid specific pitfalls, and recognize both helpful and harmful influences. The sutra emphasizes the importance of truthful observation of the nature of reality, and details practices to develop wisdom and ultimately benefit all beings.
