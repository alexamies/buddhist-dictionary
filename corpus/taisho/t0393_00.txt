<h4>Summary</h4>
This text recounts the journey of Mahākāśyapa and his disciples to the Buddha's parinirvana (death). After receiving omens and encountering a brahmin who informs them of the Buddha's passing, they travel to Kuśinagara. Upon arrival, they find the funeral pyre unlit and are told it awaits Kāśyapa, and when he arrives, the Buddha miraculously reveals his feet from the coffin, prompting expressions of grief and devotion from those present. Kāśyapa then delivers a verse praising the Buddha.
