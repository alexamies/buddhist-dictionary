<h4>Summary</h4>
The text recounts a dialogue between the Buddha and the Bodhisattva Maitreya in the kingdom of Shravasti. Maitreya asks the Buddha how a Bodhisattva can attain non-regression on the path to enlightenment, advance in the Mahayana teachings, subdue demonic forces, understand the nature of all things, and quickly achieve supreme wisdom. The Buddha praises Maitreya's question and explains that a Bodhisattva equipped with eight qualities can swiftly attain supreme enlightenment. These eight qualities include inner purity, accomplished conduct, accomplished giving, accomplished vows, compassion, loving-kindness, skillful means, and wisdom.
