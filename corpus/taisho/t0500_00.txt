Sūtra on Rāhula’s Forbearance

<h4>English Translations</h4>
None

<h4>Summary</h4>
One day, two of the Buddha’s disciples went into the city to beg for food. A disrespectful man, harboring malice, threw dirt into one’s bowl and struck the
other on the head. Despite this, their teacher reminded them of the Buddha’s teachings on patience, emphasizing that only the wise can truly endure
insults without resentment. The Buddha later explained that those who act with malice suffer dire consequences, including rebirth in hell, rebirth as
venomous creatures, and repeated misfortunes over countless lifetimes. Conversely, those who practice patience and forbearance enjoy peace, health,
prosperity, and favorable rebirths.
