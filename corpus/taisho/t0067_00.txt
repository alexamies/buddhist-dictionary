<h4>Summary</h4>
The text recounts an encounter between the Buddha's disciple, Maudgalyāyana, and a demon. The demon infiltrates Maudgalyāyana's body, but the disciple, through meditation, discovers the demon and confronts him. Maudgalyāyana then reveals a past life connection to the demon, recounting how the demon, in a previous Buddha's time, tried to corrupt monks through both persecution and false veneration, ultimately leading to his downfall and suffering in hell. The story emphasizes the importance of monks remaining steadfast in their practice, avoiding both anger and attachment to offerings, to overcome the demon's influence.
