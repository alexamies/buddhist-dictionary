Sūtra on Ānanda’s Childhood Companion

<h4>Summary</h4>
A monk named Kutta, a childhood friend of Ananda, desired to leave the monastic life due to strong desires. Ananda asked the Buddha to teach Kutta, and the Buddha described the negative aspects of women as temptations and distractions from spiritual practice. Inspired by the Buddha's words, Kutta achieved enlightenment and demonstrated his spiritual power through miraculous feats before entering Nirvana. Ananda then informed the Buddha of Kutta's passing, and the Buddha affirmed his ability to guide countless beings to liberation.
