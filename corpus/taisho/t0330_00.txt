<h4>Summary</h4>
While residing in the Jeta Grove in Shravasti, the Buddha was approached by a wealthy man, and five hundred elders seeking guidance on achieving Nirvana. They expressed their desire to follow the path of the Bodhisattva, aspiring to attain ultimate enlightenment rather than individual liberation. The Buddha then taught them the importance of cultivating boundless compassion and how Bodhisattvas should contemplate the body's impurity through forty-two contemplations to detach from worldly desires, ultimately achieving enlightenment and benefiting all beings.
