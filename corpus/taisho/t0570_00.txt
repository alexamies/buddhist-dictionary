佛說賢首經

Sage Chief Sutra
(Fo Shuo Xian Shou Jing);
Translated by Shengjian in the Western Qin in 1 scroll

<h4>Notes</h4>
Date 388-407 from Lancaster (Lancaster 2004, 'K 469')

<h4>English Translations</h4>
None

<h4>Summary</h4>
One time, the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, surrounded by a great assembly of bodhisattvas, including Maitreya, monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen,
celestial beings, dragons, spirits, and asuras. A laywoman respectfully asked the Buddha to reveal the names of Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and their lands
in the ten directions. The Buddha listed them, describing their attributes.
