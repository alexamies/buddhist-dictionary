<h4>Summary</h4>
This text is a sutra about the rules and conduct for lay Buddhist practitioners (Upasakas) who have taken the five precepts, specifically concerning the Bodhisattva precepts. It outlines many actions that constitute offenses and non-offenses. In general, a practitioner should avoid arrogance, anger, greed, harming others, and neglecting the needs of others or the dharma, with considerations for the circumstances and motivations behind the actions. The text also covers practices of devotion, ethical conduct, and the benefits of upholding the precepts for spiritual progress.
