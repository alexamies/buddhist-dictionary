<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha teaches monks about eleven thoughts to contemplate the Tathagata (Buddha), which will cultivate loving-kindness. By practicing these eleven contemplations, monks can attain certain fruits in their practice, ultimately leading to a state of liberation. Furthermore, the Buddha describes eleven benefits that arise from practicing and spreading loving-kindness, including peaceful sleep, protection from harm, and rebirth in a heavenly realm. Therefore, the monks are encouraged to diligently cultivate and spread loving-kindness.
