<h4>Summary</h4>
The text describes the origin and exposition of the twelve ascetic practices (dhuta) for monks dwelling in solitary places. The Buddha explains to Mahākāśyapa the benefits and details of these practices, which include living in remote areas, begging for food, eating only one meal a day, wearing patched robes, dwelling in cemeteries or under trees, and abstaining from lying down. The practices are meant to detach from worldly desires, purify the mind, and advance on the path to enlightenment.
