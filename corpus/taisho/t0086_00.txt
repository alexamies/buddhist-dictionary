<h4>Summary</h4>
According to the Buddha, foolish individuals suffer in life due to their evil thoughts, words, and deeds, which lead to immediate consequences in the present life through physical, mental, and emotional suffering. These individuals, who indulge in harmful actions and lack faith in Buddha, Dharma, and karma, are destined to be reborn in "Niraya" (hell), a realm of unimaginable torment. In Niraya, beings endure horrific punishments such as being impaled, burned, dismembered, and devoured by creatures, experiencing agony beyond comprehension. The sutra emphasizes that only by abandoning evil and embracing virtuous conduct can one avoid such a dreadful fate.
