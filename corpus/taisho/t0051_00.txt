<h4>English Translation</h4>
From the Pāli Vatthūpamasūtta (Majjhima Nikāya 7)

<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha, after achieving enlightenment, encounters a Brahman who believes in purification through water rituals. The Buddha explains that true purification comes from abandoning 21 defilements that lead to suffering and cultivating virtues like loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity. He emphasizes that external cleansing is ineffective against inner impurities, and that virtuous conduct is the path to true liberation. The Brahman, convinced by the Buddha's teachings, takes refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, vowing to abstain from killing.
