<h4>Summary</h4>
The Buddha, while at Jetavana Vihara in Shravasti, spoke to a gathering of monks, nuns, laymen and laywomen about the Mani-rocana Sutra to alleviate the suffering of the people. The sutra lists names of Buddhas and various malevolent ghosts and spirits that cause illnesses and misfortune. By reciting the names of these ghosts and the Mani-rocana Sutra, people can break the power of these spirits and recover from sickness. The Buddha assures the assembly that these words are from the mouth of all the Buddhas and that all will be healed upon hearing the sutra.
