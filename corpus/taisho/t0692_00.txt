<h4>Summary</h4>
The text describes a conversation between the Buddha and King Udayana, a young ruler eager to learn about the benefits of creating Buddha images. The Buddha explains that making Buddha images leads to auspicious rebirths, characterized by physical beauty, wealth, noble status, and ultimately, the path to Nirvana. The king and his ministers, delighted by this teaching, pay homage to the Buddha and are later reborn in Amitabha's Pure Land.
