<h4>Summary</h4>
While the Buddha was residing in a certain kingdom, Nandiśvara, a monk, expressed distress upon learning that the Buddha would soon be leaving. The Buddha responded by advising Nandiśvara to cultivate five inner qualities: faith, purity, generosity, wisdom, and learning, and to practice six types of mindfulness. These six contemplations involve reflecting on the virtues of the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha, moral precepts, acts of giving, and the qualities of the heavens. By focusing on these virtues, one can overcome negative emotions, attain inner peace, and realize the Four Noble Truths.
